What is paper marbling?

The images used on this website are taken from The Art of marbling as applied to paper (1881) by C.W. Woolnough. I've always thought that marbled bookends on old books are very beautiful and striking. When trying to come up with a design for this website, I wanted a banner that was relatively abstract, but colourful and ideally with some Victorian flair (I am, unfortunately, a 19th century historian). I then remembered paper marbling and came across Woolnough's book and its wonderful illustrations on Wellcome Collection's online archive.

Paper marbling is a print making technique, which (as the name suggests) produces images that look like colourful marble. It was first developed in Japan around the 10th century, but it gradually spread throughout Asia and (eventually) to Europe. In Europe, paper marbling was particularly popular as a way to decorate bookends. In England specifically, Woolnough's book popularised paper marbling as a handicraft that could be practiced at home. The digitised edition of his book (linked above) containes detailed instructions for how to recreate its designs.
